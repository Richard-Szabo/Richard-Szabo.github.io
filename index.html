ABOUT

Zoltán Katona

Zoltán Katona (Oroszlány, Hungary) after graduating from the Dance and Art School of Győr and having obtained a dance diploma, he worked with several prestigious professional dancing companies around the world in Europe, Hungary, Spain, the United States, Canada, Austria. Some to mention, National Ballet Company of Győr (Hungary), Ballet de Teatres de la Generalitat Valenciana (Spain), Projecto TITOYAYA (Spain) with Gustavo Ramírez Sansano, Eva Bertomeu Cia Danza (Spain), Joshua Cienfuegos (Spain), Luna Negra Dance Theater (USA) Gustavo Ramirez Sansano.

He has worked with many famous choreographers such as Jiří Kylián, Nacho Duato, Gustavo Ramírez Sansano, Patrick de Bana, Ben van Cauwenbergh, Dimitrij Simkin, Béla Földi, Asun Noales, Ramon Oller, Fernando Melo, Luis Eduardo Sayago, Fernando Hernando Magadan and many more … In 2008 he received the “Best Ballet Master of the Year” award (IDO – International Dance Organization). In 2009, he won the Best Performance Award for Pure Pleasure Choreography (Choreographed and performed with Mónica Cervantes Rodriguez and MouDansa) at the Burgos-New York World Cup Championship in the Canary Islands. As an assistant, rehearsal director and choreographer, he continues his work. He is teaching contemporary and classical ballet in different countries, including Hungary, Austria, Spain, United States of America, Cuba, Croatia … Main choreographies: “House” for Luna Negra Dance Theater (Chicago, USA), Game I. Győr Ballet Dance Company, Game II . Hungarian National Dance Festival Győr, Beginning – Summer Intensive Course for Luna Negra Dance Theater,

‘MásKépMás’ – Többlettél Project closing performance and the choreography started Day 0 the first time in the 12th Hungarian Dance Festival opening night.

Personality – Dance and Fine Arts School of Győr graduated performance for the third act on their exam concerto …

“Unattainable” choreography at the Dances Olympic World Championship in Berlin – Gold Medal – 1st place in Contemporary Male Solo category 17 years Glaser Marton who was a student of Győr Dance and Fine Arts School.

Worked as a PLC, industrial camera programmer.

Now working as a freelance artist, creating photos, videos, moves…
